Satsukikaze Works

"Satsukikaze Works" Project is an anime/game merchandise project. Launching the "Satsukikaze Works" brand selling unique nixie tube clocks made with my own hands.

This project started with market research and a feasibility check. I had found similar products at very high prices, with demand from a small niche of fans.

The next step was cost calculation and the selection of suppliers. Selecting suppliers from all over the world and trying to reduce material costs in order to lower the selling price without affecting product quality.

The last step was building a web shop and advertising it. Created a web shop on booth.pm (the biggest Japanese handmade merchandise site) and registered a Twitter account for advertising.

Cinetic Bikes

Cinetic Bikes was a fixed bike brand.

The first thing I did was to look for someone who could help me with this project. I found a friend of mine who is a bicycle lover. With him we started thinking about what bike would be easy to build, aesthetically beautiful, and at an affordable price. So we started searching the Internet to find a bike frame supplier. Having found our supplier, the next step was to find all the other components of the bike. taking cost in mind, we were able to get all the components.

The most exciting step was the assembly. seeing our bike take shape. Whoa.. While we were waiting for the paint job, I worked on the name and logo for the bike. "Cinetic Bikes", the Logo came out great. We also wanted to do the dedicated website but there was a problem. we didn't know how to do it. and that's where I started thinking about Boolean... For the site we had decided to give up on it, and start selling it on a popular used bike website.

After a few days, our first bike was sold. Yes! but a little too long to sell one... We forgot about advertising on social media! we didn't even have the budget for that... they were not in our costs estimations. this was a huge mistake.

Anyway, we were happy!

But here comes the biggest problem with the project. We had no time. We were high school students, just before the graduation exam, and it was not easy to find all the time for this. So we decided to drop the project and leave it as an experience for our future.

I learned how to talk to suppliers, organize the work, think about how to improve the product, which are the demands, and how advertising is important for a brand.

This helped me a lot for my future projects.
